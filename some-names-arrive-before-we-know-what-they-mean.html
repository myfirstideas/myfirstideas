The small poetry of invented words

Invented words can feel strangely intimate. They are assembled, but not random. They are built to sound inevitable after the fact. That may be why they linger. They create the illusion that they have always existed, waiting to be discovered.

The way it seems to promise order in a life that rarely stays orderly for long.

Not every polished name deserves the power it’s given. But many of them reveal something worth noticing about the rest of us. We are listeners before we are analysts. We respond to rhythm, suggestion, and the faint glow of certainty.
