Familiarity has its own gravity

One of the least discussed forces in any practical decision is the pull of what already feels known. People build tiny loyalties around ordinary tools and routines. So when two options seem close, the question is not always, "Which one is better?" Sometimes the real question is, "Which one lets life keep its shape?" That is a subtler, more human question. It belongs less to advertising and more to habit.
