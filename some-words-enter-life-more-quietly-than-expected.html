When language crosses into routine

There’s something culturally revealing about how quickly technical vocabulary becomes ordinary speech. Once a term enters daily life, it stops behaving like specialist language. It becomes shorthand within a household. Someone says the name, and they are no longer referring only to an item or a category. They may be referring to a phase of life, a change in routine, a period of adjustment, or simply the fact that bodies and minds do not always move through the world as predictably as people wish.

That shift is easy to miss. We often imagine healthcare language as belonging to institutions, but so much of it ends up living in intimate spaces. The formal and the personal start sharing a room.

Familiarity is stranger than expertise
